Fruit Ripeness Indicator Using Anthocyanin

Developed a natural, pH-sensitive detection system to assess fruit ripeness using anthocyanin extracted from red cabbage. This bio-indicator reacts visibly to ethylene gas emitted by fruits during the ripening process, enabling color-based assessment of ripeness stages. The project combined principles of chemistry and plant biology to create a cost-effective and environmentally friendly solution for monitoring produce freshness. Experimental trials were conducted on various fruits, and results were recorded using a color calibration chart, demonstrating clear and measurable shifts in pigmentation correlated with ripening.

Project Highlights

  • Extracted anthocyanin from red cabbage through a controlled boiling and filtration process
  • Created pH-sensitive test strips that change color in response to ethylene gas
  • Tested the system with various fruits including bananas, apples, and tomatoes
  • Documented color changes at different ripeness stages
  • Achieved 85% accuracy in ripeness detection compared to traditional methods
  • Received Honorable Mention at the State Science Fair for innovation and sustainability

Scientific Process

The project involved extracting anthocyanin compounds through a careful chemical process, creating standardized test conditions, and validating results through multiple trials. I learned about organic chemistry, pH indicators, and plant biology while developing this practical application.
